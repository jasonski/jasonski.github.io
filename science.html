That's the result of our back-projection attempt at the Basel EGS - a planed geothermal powerplant project which was closed down because of the occurence of a M3.4 event in 2006. The observation here was that all four M>3 events appear to rupture backward into the direction of the stress perturbation - the borehole.

Directivity of about 80 M<3 events from the Basel EGS site in 2006. The directivity of the red events was calculated by back-projection. The directivity of the other events was obtained by fitting the peak amplitudes of their reconstructed source time functions with all possible directivity radiation patterns.

Reconstructed source-time functions for two events at four different stations showing the complexity of the rupture (not a single simple burst). The green and blue shaded peaks show different bursts of released moment and are indicative of multiple rupture phases or repeated rupture.

A mother and daughter event were used for spectral ratio analysis. The parent had a complex source time function. Here are her seismograms at OT2, where the secondary event (rupture phase) can be seen nicely in both the p- and s-phase time windows.

Spectral ratio method to extract the source spectrum of the larger event of an event pair. The obtained corner frequency can be used to estimate the source size and the stress drop.

Stress drop depth slice from the northern Chilean subduction zone. Blue colors correspond to low, red colors to high stress drop. The most interesting aspect here is the slight but distinct difference of median stress drop with location. Interface stress drops are low, while upper plate stress drops are higher. The intermediate depth event stress drops show a dependence on distance from the plate surface.

A repeating series of earthquakes from northern Chile. The events show an extremely high similarity in waveform, even though they occur in different years. For this to be the case, their location, size and mechanism must be almost identical. With a filter of 1-5Hz, the naked eye cannot tell the difference between the events in this series.

Repeating earthakes from northern Chile. A shows the cumulative moment of 50 repeater sequences against time. The vertical black line is the 2014 MW8.1 Iquiue event. B displays the high precision relocation of one repeater group demontrating the "only" partial overlap of their rupture areas.
